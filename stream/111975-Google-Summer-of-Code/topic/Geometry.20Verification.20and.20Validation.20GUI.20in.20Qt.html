Divyanshu Garg (May 01 2026 at 09:33):

Another important aspect of contribution would be communication,

  • Be clear about the availability; it doesn't have to be like a 24/7 task, but as a simple example, if you are gonna be away for a day, week, or any period of time due to exams, travel, or any other potential reason, inform them beforehand.
  • This creates a sense of clarity and trust, since in opposite scenarios, it creates problems for the ORG to calculate the progress and continuation of the project. Example - Disappearing for 15 days without notice might give mentors the impression that the contributor abandoned the GSoC program mid-way. It might sound absurd, but trust me, we have seen this happening a lot in recent years. (In terms of recent generation its called ghosting)
  • Never hesitate to ask questions, remember GSoC efforts are focused on a few agendas, one being to help new contributors learn.
  • Sharing Updates - can play a very good role in keeping the conversation active and letting the ORG and mentors know what's happening around the project.
  • You can choose to put casual updates regularly or choose an SOD or EOD plan to give yourself daily goals and see if you were able to achieve those goals.
